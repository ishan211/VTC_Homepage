Frequently asked questions

How fast can I get a decision brief?

VTConnect delivers drafts in approximately 72 hours, with final packages within one week for most scopes. Timeline varies based on data availability and complexity. Pattern Recognition Workshops follow an 8-week cycle from Letter of Intent to implementation support.

Do you support privacy and OCAP® principles?

Yes. We follow government-grade security and OCAP®-aligned practices for stewardship and consent. Our platform operates on a Sovereign Canadian Data Lake with strict data governance protocols. All data integration is done securely with full transparency about sources and confidence levels.

Can I bring my own data?

Absolutely—internal sources can be securely joined with our Social Infrastructure Database (SID) and community inputs, with transparent citations and confidence levels. We specialize in connecting your local data with 500+ verified external sources to reveal cross-domain patterns.

What makes VTC different from traditional consulting?

Traditional consulting examines sectors in isolation. VTC's Diagonal Integration shows how they overlap—how a school closure alters transit ridership, how hospital expansion shifts housing demand. We make these hidden connections visible, measurable, and actionable with evidence-based confidence levels.

How do I know if Pattern Recognition Workshop is right for my organization?

If your organization manages public infrastructure, serves across multiple departments, or faces the "Two-Books Problem" where different agencies reach opposite conclusions using verified data, our workshop can help. It's designed for municipalities, school districts, health authorities, and higher education institutions. The risk-free model means no cost if grant funding isn't approved.

What's the difference between beta and full platform access?

Beta access (October 2025 - December 2025) offers evidence packages priced between $1,000-$2,000 for Metro Vancouver, with founding partners receiving permanent discounted rates. Full platform launch (Q1 2026) includes annual subscriptions ($1,000-$2,000) with unlimited basic queries, premium reports, and API access for enterprise partners.

What kind of results have you achieved so far?

Current network performance includes $2.1B in assets identified as at-risk, $280M in annual funding opportunities mapped, 500+ validated patterns discovered, 70% of patterns spanning across departments, and 15% average efficiency gain post-workshop. Work with the Vancouver School Board revealed a 15,000-student enrollment gap between district and ministry counts.

How does the grant funding process work?

We handle all grant complexity and administration. After you submit a Letter of Intent, we identify Infrastructure Canada programs, prepare applications, and align funding. These grants typically cover 100% of workshop costs. If funding is not secured, there is no fee charged to your organization.
